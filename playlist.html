The title itself explains the concept of this playlist very clearly, it is made up with two parts: bisexual and black turtleneck.

Bisexuality here represents an openness and possibilities. When we talk about stereotypes of queer people, it is not always ethical but proven by experiences that there does exist a connection between how people present themselves and their sexuality, since looks and visual presentation plays a pivotal role in the stories we experience. As a society, we developed words and languages to describe those certain stereotypes. But how do we describe bisexuality by how they represent themselves? Not so many words to describe, I thought. So back to the start, I thought bisexuality exists more because of an openness, because restrictions don't exist on gender but because they chose their partner based upon other qualities or overall energies.

I really like this idea, I agree that attraction based both on intellectual intimacy and physical intimacy benefits more than those who lack enough intellectual bonds. And in this case, I think bisexuality or pansexuality would represent this idea better than both straight and homosexuals, directly or metaphorically.

The next part is the black turtleneck. To me it represents sexiness, mystery and power dynamics that’s hidden. Purely wearing a black turtleneck wouldn’t mean anything, but it smokes attraction and invents you to explore. It is nothing and everything at the same time.

Together combined, “a bisexual’s black turtleneck" paints the picture of a private open invitation, that’s sexy.

by Cosima Phoenix
